PORTFOLIO

Challenges

-KiZo is a smart parking app that allows people to find parking spaces as efficiently as possible.
-The app works by helping people locate registered parking spaces nearest to their destination, and providing the option to reserve them in advance.

Objectives

-Make sure users feel satisfy with their options after completing the app.
-Find the nearest, cheapest and bestest parking spots.

         Persona



- Fictional users whose goals and characteristics represent the needs of a larger group of users.
- This persona become the key to the design process because it reflects the lifestyles of users and give my team an idea of how to meet users’ needs or challenges.
- The design ensures users to have the best experience while using the product since these information speak directly to users.

         Sticky Notes



- Based on the theme that: Most participants feel that they don’t know how many spaces are available in the parking lot. An insight is: users can check availability of the spot while they’re selecting the spaces.
- Based on the theme that: Most participants are confused about whether their parking spots have been reserved or not. An insight is: users feel relieved since there is a pop-up screen that says “spot has successfully booked”.
- Based on the theme that: Many participants feel lost when checking the parking lot. An insight is: users are surprised that they’re able to view locations of the parking lots by viewing the map inside the app.

         Close-up Storyboard



- Visualize potential solutions to problems that users are facing, describes and explores a user’s experience with a product
The storyboard contains six panels:
- In panel one, Leo struggles to find a parking spot.
- Next, Leo decides to try Kizo app.
- In the third panel, Leo learns how to use KiZo and books a parking spot.
- In the fourth panel, Leo drives to the spot that he has reserved.
- In the fifth panel, Leo has successfully parked his car at the given spot.
- Last panel, Leo feels happy that he has saved so much time after using Kizo.

         Paper Wireframes



- Establish the basic structure of a page and highlight the intended function of each element.
- Translating hand-drawn wireframes into digital wireframes.
- Paper wireframes are the fastest way to get your idea out, and it is inexpensive.

         High-Fidelity Prototypes



- Representation of a final product that demonstrates its functionality.
- A series of interconnected mockups that represent all the pages in a design.
- Demonstrates how the products look, feel and work before passing it down to the development team.
